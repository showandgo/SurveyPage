Introduction

Heʻeia today is one of the most intact and functioning ahupuaʻa systems across Hawaiʻi, even as parts of Oʻahu have overwhelmingly become developed. The bioculturally restored lands today honors the traditions of Hawaiian cultural practices, and provides much physical, relational and spiritual nourishment Many hold deep familial and relational connections to this land, which will be lost if developed. There are always pressures to develop such lands for commercial profit, and many hands have contributed tirelessly and invested tremendously over the past 25 years to maintain and expand these conservation lands. To restore the ahupuaʻa to a place where it is once again abundant with life, back to a land that nourishes and feeds in more ways than one, back to a land that will serve the community and all future generations to come.
